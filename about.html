A small private sentence pinned to a real place. Pinned is an ongoing, slowly-growing portrait of San Francisco told through small private answers to a single question. Every two weeks a new question is posted, and people answer with one sentence, pinned to a real place in the city.

Over time the map fills with strangers' Sunday mornings, sleepless walks, first kisses, and quiet places. It becomes a city remembered through its emotional geography rather than its landmarks. You can read every answer ever pinned. You can only add your own — never edit, reply to, or react to someone else's. The silence between strangers is part of the project.

The "assignment" format is borrowed lovingly from Learning to Love You More, a project by Harrell Fletcher and Miranda July (2002–2009), acquired in 2010 by the San Francisco Museum of Modern Art. Their seventy assignments asked strangers to make small, specific, often tender things — and trusted the strangers to make them. Pinned takes that same trust and points it at one city.
